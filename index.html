Biography

Hi there! My name is Tyler and I’m currently a graduate student in the University of California, Berkeley Astrophysics program completing his Master’s degree in May 2022. Prior to joining the Berkeley Astronomy Department, I completed my Bachelor of Science degrees in Physics and Astrophysics at Arizona State University.

For the past 4 years, I’ve served as a research assistant with the HERA collaboration using Python (Numpy, Pandas, Scikit-Learn, Scipy, Tensorflow) and SQL to perform statistical modeling and data analysis on terabyte-sized datasets to detect the first galaxies in the universe.

My professional interests lie in creative problem solving through reproducible and auditable programming, with a major focus on deciphering large and complex data structures to reveal the insight hidden within. I have experience working with Python, SQL (Hive), Hadoop, Excel, Tableau, advanced data visualization, and machine learning techniques to extract insight and effectively communicate results.

I am actively looking for data analytics/science positions beginning in June 2022.
